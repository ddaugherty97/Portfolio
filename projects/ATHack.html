MIT Assistive Tech Hackathon

March 2019

I along with 5 other teammates attended the MIT Assistive Technology Hackathon in March of 2019. Our team worked with Alex who shared with us his strong passion for spending time with his family, especially his two highly active sons. Alex came to us with a desire to have an attachment for his wheelchair that would allow him to play catch with his sons more easily. He noted that at this young age it is an important time for his sons to develop a passion for sports, and as a previous professional ultimate frisbee athlete, Alex really wants to be involved in helping his sons develop this passion.

Over the course of 12 hours, we co-designed with Alex to create a modular slingshot attachment to help him play catch with his sons. At the MIT's Beaverworks Lab, we had access to prototyping materials such as PVC tubing and aluminum stock to build our prototype.

The slingshot consisted of a PVC frame that could slide into the mounting attachments located on the armrests of Alex's chair. The launcher was created out of bent aluminum tubing and uses TRX exercise bands to launch the ball up to 20 feet.

At the end of the day, our team was awared 2nd place at the Hackathon for in the category for Co-Designer Collaboration, and Alex went back home with our completed slingshot attachment. We were so happy to see this video sent to us the next morning.

For more information about this project, check out the documentation on MIT's ATHack Website!
